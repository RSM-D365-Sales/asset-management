How to use HarborMaster

An offline-first asset & maintenance workspace for marine fleets, backed by Dynamics 365 F&SCM Enterprise Asset Management. This guide walks through every screen — including how to build your own report live.

1.Getting started

Open the application in any modern browser — there is nothing to install.

  • The app ships with a fully seeded fleet: 8 tugs, a year of completed maintenance history, engine hour logs, open work, and a forward drydock plan.
  • Everything is stored locally in your browser first and synchronized to D365 when connected — so it keeps working at sea. See Working offline.
  • The sidebar on the left is the main navigation; the search bar at the top finds assets by name, location, or class.

2.Dashboard

Your day at a glance: tasks assigned to you, fleet status, and pinned announcements.

  • Work orders due today appear at the top — click one to jump straight into its checklist.
  • Pinned announcements (e.g. updated lockout/tagout procedures) stay visible until acknowledged.

3.Assets & logging hours

Assets lists every tug, dock, and barge with live tank gauges and open-work counts. Click a vessel to open its detail page.

On the asset detail page

  • Equipment tree — functional locations down to components (main engines, generators, winch…).
  • Component hour log — record an hour-meter reading; readings drive the preventive-maintenance triggers and the projection charts in Insight Tables.
  • Gauges — potable water, fuel, oil, and grey water with warning thresholds.
Try it: log new engine hours on Apollo, then open the usage-projection insight table — the projected service due dates shift in real time, because the system learns the boat's actual operating tempo.

4.Work orders & checklists

Preventive routines and corrective jobs, executed as low-click checklists.

  1. Open Work Orders and pick a job (filter by status or search).
  2. Work through the steps: yes/no items get Pass Fail Flag; gauge steps capture a reading on a slider.
  3. Need the procedure? The PDF job aid link is on every work order — no per-step sign-off required.
  4. Add labor time and cost category before completing, so the hours land in reporting.

Failing or flagging a step can spin up a deficiency without leaving the checklist.

5.Work requests (deficiencies)

Anything found broken on rounds gets logged here, triaged, and tracked to resolution.

  • Click New Request, pick the asset/component, set priority, and describe the finding.
  • Each request keeps a full timeline — logged, triaged, parts ordered, resolved.

6.Insight Tables

Saved cross-fleet summary reports built from everything the crews capture. Open one from the list — every table supports Export Table (CSV).

  • Engineering: Caterpillar / EMD Summary — every engine of that manufacturer fleet-wide: current hours, last completion, and when each service comes due. Overdue items show in red.
  • Engine Usage & PM Projection — charts each engine's logged hours, fits the observed usage rate, and marks the date the trend crosses each service threshold.
  • Engine Hours Log — every meter reading, newest first.
  • PM Status / Open Deficiencies — work-order and deficiency rollups by category and vessel.

7.Drydock Schedule

A half-month planning board for tentatively scheduling yard periods across the fleet, a year ahead.

  1. Each row is a tug; each column is half a month (1–15 / 16–31).
  2. Click any cell to cycle the plan: H in service → DD planned drydock → RDD regulatory drydock → OOS out of service → back to H.
  3. Watch the left columns: COI expiration and DD Expire turn amber, then red, as deadlines approach — and the period containing a drydock deadline gets a red ring on the grid.
  4. The right-hand column totals each boat's in-service months.
Planning tip: pair yard windows with upcoming engine services from the Insight Tables — one haul-out, both jobs.

8.Analytics — build a report in front of your audience

An ad-hoc, drag-and-drop report canvas. Think Power BI field wells, computed live from the local data.

Build your first report

  1. Open Analytics. The palette at the top offers five widgets: KPI Card, Bar Chart, Trend Line, Donut, and Table.
  2. Drag a widget onto the canvas — or simply click it. It lands already showing real data.
  3. Shape it with the dropdowns on the widget:
    • Dataset — Work Orders, Labor & Cost, Deficiencies, Hour Readings, Drydock Plan, or Fleet.
    • Measure — count, labor hours, estimated hours, fleet running hours…
    • Group by — asset, category, worker, priority, builder…
    • Filter — open only, completed only, corrective only, contract labor…
  4. Rename the title by typing over it, resize with the expand icon, reorder by dragging the grip handle, remove with ×.

Example: "Which boats eat the most labor?"

  1. Add a Bar Chart.
  2. Dataset → Labor & Cost, Measure → Labor hours, Group by → Asset.
  3. Add a Trend Line, switch its filter to Corrective only — is unplanned work trending up?

Your canvas is saved automatically and will be exactly as you left it next time you open the app.

9.Working offline

Crews work at sea; HarborMaster assumes the connection will drop.

  • Every edit — checklist results, hour logs, deficiencies, drydock plans — is saved locally first and queued for synchronization.
  • The Online / Offline pill in the top-right shows connection state and the count of queued changes; everything replays to D365 automatically when the connection returns.
  • To see it in action, click the pill to simulate going offline, make some edits, then click it again — watch the queue drain.
  • After your first visit the app is fully cached, so it even loads with no connection at all.
